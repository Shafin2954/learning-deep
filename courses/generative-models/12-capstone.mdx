## The project

You will implement three generative models — a VAE, a DDPM, and a simple GAN — trained on
the same small image dataset (MNIST, FashionMNIST, or CIFAR-10). You will evaluate all three
with FID or KID and write a structured tradeoff analysis comparing sample quality, training
stability, exact likelihood, and sampling speed. The goal is not to match state-of-the-art
numbers; it is to prove you can build these systems from their mathematical definitions.

## Hard constraints (no vibe coding)

- You MAY NOT use any pre-built VAE, GAN, or diffusion library for the core model
  (no PyTorch Lightning VAE helpers, no torchgan, no diffusers UNet2DModel for the denoiser).
- You MUST implement the reparameterization trick, ELBO computation, noise schedule, and
  DDPM sampling loop manually.
- Your diffusion denoiser must be your own architecture — a small U-Net or MLP you define
  yourself. You MAY use `nn.Conv2d`, `nn.Linear`, etc.
- You MUST use the same dataset and the same train/test split for all three models.
- You MAY use `torchmetrics` for FID/KID computation.
- You MAY use `torch.optim.Adam` and standard DataLoaders.

## What it must contain (and the chapter each tests)

- **VAE** (ch. 3): encoder $q_\phi(z|x)$ outputs $\mu$ and $\log\sigma^2$; reparameterization
  $z = \mu + \sigma \odot \epsilon$, $\epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I)$; ELBO loss =
  reconstruction + closed-form KL; sample from the prior $z \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I)$ and
  decode; interpolate between two latent codes and show the smooth transition.
- **DDPM — noise schedule** (ch. 7): implement both a linear schedule ($\beta_1 = 10^{-4}$
  to $\beta_T = 0.02$, $T = 1000$) and a cosine schedule; precompute $\alpha_t$, $\bar\alpha_t$,
  and the closed-form $q(x_t|x_0)$ for arbitrary $t$.
- **DDPM — training and sampling** (ch. 8): simplified loss
  $\mathcal{L} = \|\epsilon - \epsilon_\theta(x_t, t)\|^2$; reverse sampling loop
  $x_{t-1} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\alpha_t}}(x_t - \frac{1-\alpha_t}{\sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}}\epsilon_\theta(x_t,t)) + \sigma_t z$;
  sample a grid of images from noise.
- **GAN** (ch. 4–5): DCGAN-style generator and discriminator; non-saturating generator
  loss $-\mathbb{E}[\log D(G(z))]$; alternating update; at least 20 epochs of training;
  show generated samples.
- **Evaluation** (ch. 11): compute FID or KID on 1 000+ generated images vs the test set
  for each model; report in a comparison table.
- **Tradeoff analysis** (written, no code required): compare VAE vs GAN vs DDPM on all
  four dimensions: sample quality, training stability, exact likelihood availability,
  sampling speed (seconds per image or batch).

## Acceptance criteria

- VAE ELBO decreases monotonically and converges; ELBO on the test set is reported.
- VAE samples from the prior are recognizable as belonging to the training distribution
  (not random noise); latent interpolation shows smooth transitions.
- DDPM sampling from random Gaussian noise produces recognizable images after the full
  reverse chain; show a grid of at least 16 samples.
- GAN trains without complete mode collapse: generated samples show variety across at
  least 5 distinct classes or modes.
- FID or KID is reported for all three models on the same dataset and test split.
- The tradeoff analysis addresses: which model produces the best FID, which is most
  stable to train, which gives exact NLL, which is fastest to sample from.
