## The project

Take a raw, messy dataset and build a fully reproducible, leak-proof analysis pipeline that
moves from raw data to a trained model with a CV score proven to match the holdout. Every
data decision — what to impute, how to encode, which features to engineer — must be
documented with a rationale. The deliverable is not just a model: it is a risk register that
lists every potential leakage source and the mitigation taken, plus adversarial validation
confirming that train and test distributions are compatible.

## Hard constraints (no vibe coding)

- **No data leakage of any kind.** Every transformation that computes a statistic from the
  data (mean, std, target mean, category frequency, scaling bounds) must be computed inside a
  Pipeline or computed only on the training fold.
- **Adversarial validation is required.** If your adversarial AUC exceeds 0.55, you must
  investigate and fix the cause before calling the result valid.
- **No AI writes your EDA narrative or your feature decisions.** You must author the written
  sections yourself; the whole point is to develop judgment about data.
- **Every transform must be inside a Pipeline.** There must be no standalone calls to
  `scaler.fit_transform(X)` outside a fitted Pipeline or CV loop.

## What it must contain (and the chapter each tests)

1. **Full EDA report** (ch. 5–7): distribution plots for all numeric features (histogram +
   box plot); countplot or value counts for all categorical features; a missing-value heatmap;
   a Pearson/Spearman correlation matrix with annotations; Mutual Information scores for all
   features against the target. Document what you find at each step.

2. **Documented missing-data strategy per column** (ch. 4): for each column with missing
   values, state which mechanism you believe applies (MCAR / MAR / MNAR), which imputation
   strategy you chose, and why. If you chose to drop rows, state the fraction dropped and
   your reasoning.

3. **Outlier handling** (ch. 5): apply IQR fencing or modified z-score to identify outliers
   in numeric columns. For each outlier group, decide and document: clip, remove, flag as a
   binary feature, or leave. Verify your decision by comparing CV score with and without the
   treatment.

4. **Feature engineering** (ch. 8–10): apply at least one numeric transform (log or power),
   at least one categorical encoding strategy (one-hot, ordinal, target, or frequency), at
   least one datetime feature (if a date column exists), and at least one cyclical feature
   (hour-of-day or month). Document why you chose each transform for each column.

5. **Leak-proof sklearn Pipeline with ColumnTransformer** (ch. 14): build a complete
   preprocessing Pipeline that handles all numeric and categorical branches, followed by a
   model. Verify leak-free fit by extracting a transformer parameter (e.g., imputer mean)
   from the fitted Pipeline and confirming it matches the training-fold statistic, not the
   full-dataset statistic.

6. **CV scheme that matches your holdout distribution** (ch. 12–13): choose stratified
   k-fold, GroupKFold, or TimeSeriesSplit depending on the nature of the data. Run adversarial
   validation; confirm AUC < 0.55. If AUC ≥ 0.55, identify the offending features and
   either remove them or adjust the split strategy.

7. **Leakage risk register**: a written list of every potential leakage source identified
   during your analysis, with the mitigation you applied. Include at minimum: target leakage
   candidates (any feature that could be computed from the outcome), preprocessing leakage
   (how you ensured the scaler/imputer was not fit on test data), temporal leakage (if the
   data is time-ordered), and group leakage (if entities repeat across rows).

## Acceptance criteria

- Your CV score (mean ± std across 5 folds) overlaps with your holdout score — the holdout
  falls within the CV mean ± 2 std interval.
- Adversarial validation AUC is below 0.55, confirming train and test are drawn from the same
  distribution.
- Your Pipeline can be pickled and reloaded (`joblib.dump` / `joblib.load`), and the reloaded
  model produces bit-identical predictions on the test set.
- A manual check confirms: fitting the scaler on the training fold only. Extract
  `pipe.named_steps['prep'].named_transformers_['num'].named_steps['sc'].mean_` and verify it
  equals `X_train['numeric_col'].mean()` to within 1e-6.
- All categorical imputation and scaling fit only on training data — verified by running the
  Pipeline inside a CV loop and checking that each fold produces a different fitted scaler
  mean.

## Skills checklist (tick before you claim done)

- [ ] I can classify each missing-value pattern as MCAR, MAR, or MNAR and defend my classification with evidence from the data.
- [ ] I can detect outliers with both IQR fencing and modified z-score, and I can articulate the difference in robustness.
- [ ] I can build a leak-safe target encoding using out-of-fold means and explain why the naive version leaks.
- [ ] I can construct a ColumnTransformer with separate numeric and categorical branches and verify the fit happened only on training data.
- [ ] I can run adversarial validation, interpret the result, and identify which feature is causing distribution shift when AUC is high.
- [ ] I can choose the correct CV scheme (stratified, group, or temporal) for a given problem and articulate the consequence of using the wrong one.
- [ ] I can apply cyclical encoding for time features and explain why raw hour/month integers give a linear model incorrect distance information.
- [ ] I can write and use a leakage risk register that covers all four leakage types: target, temporal, preprocessing, and group.
