## The project

Build a self-contained RL algorithm suite that progresses from tabular methods to deep RL
on two environments: a custom gridworld and CartPole. The goal is to prove — by implementing
every algorithm from scratch — that you understand the distinction between value-based, policy-based,
and model-based methods, and the role each component plays in modern deep RL.

## Hard constraints (no vibe coding)

- No RL libraries (Stable-Baselines3, RLlib, CleanRL, tianshou, or similar). You may use only
  the environment API (`gymnasium` for CartPole; your own gridworld class).
- No pre-built replay buffers, advantage estimators, or policy classes from external packages.
- No copying algorithm implementations verbatim. Every component must be yours.
- No AI-generated boilerplate. You must be able to explain every line on request.
- PyTorch is allowed for neural networks. NumPy is allowed everywhere.

## What it must contain (and the chapter each tests)

- **Gridworld implementation**: a configurable maze environment with start, goal, optional walls,
  and stochastic transitions (p=0.1 slip). (ch. 1–2)
- **Value iteration**: compute $V^*$ and $\pi^*$ analytically using the gridworld's known model. (ch. 3)
- **Q-learning and SARSA**: tabular agents on the gridworld; compare convergence speed and
  on-policy vs off-policy behaviour under the stochastic transition. (ch. 4, 6)
- **Monte Carlo control**: episodic first-visit MC on the gridworld; compare sample efficiency
  vs Q-learning at equal episode counts. (ch. 5)
- **n-step TD**: implement $n$-step SARSA for $n \in \{1, 3, 10\}$ and plot the learning curves. (ch. 7)
- **DQN on CartPole**: experience replay buffer, target network with periodic hard update,
  $\epsilon$-greedy decay; track mean episode reward over 500 episodes. (ch. 9)
- **REINFORCE on CartPole**: vanilla policy gradient with a baseline; compare variance to DQN. (ch. 10)
- **PPO on CartPole**: GAE advantage estimation, clipped surrogate objective, 4-epoch mini-batch
  updates per rollout; must reach mean reward ≥ 450 within 200 iterations. (ch. 11)
- **Sample-efficiency comparison**: a single plot showing episodes/steps to reach 90% of the
  optimal return for each algorithm on its respective environment. (ch. 1–11)

## Acceptance criteria

- Value iteration: $V^*$ converges in ≤ 100 iterations on a 6×6 gridworld.
- Tabular Q-learning: reaches optimal policy (shortest-path) in ≤ 300 episodes on 5×5 gridworld.
- DQN: mean episode reward ≥ 400 on CartPole-v1 within 500 training episodes.
- PPO: mean episode reward ≥ 450 on CartPole-v1 within 200 iterations (n_steps=128).
- REINFORCE returns are at least 50% of DQN's at the same step count (variance is expected
  to be higher; this criterion checks correctness, not performance).
- Sample-efficiency plot is reproducible with a fixed random seed.
- All components (replay buffer, GAE, policy network, target net update) are less than 300 lines
  total, excluding the gridworld environment class.

## Skills checklist (tick before you claim done)

- [ ] I can implement the Bellman optimality equation as a matrix operation.
- [ ] I can explain the on-policy vs off-policy distinction using SARSA and Q-learning on
  the same stochastic environment.
- [ ] I can implement experience replay and explain why it breaks the Markovian correlation
  problem in DQN.
- [ ] I can implement GAE with a configurable $\lambda$ and explain the bias-variance trade-off
  at $\lambda=0$ vs $\lambda=1$.
- [ ] I can implement the PPO clip objective and explain geometrically what the clip does to
  the gradient.
- [ ] I can produce a fair sample-efficiency comparison (controlling for environment steps,
  not episodes).
- [ ] I can run any of the above algorithms with a different random seed and get results within
  ±10% of the acceptance criteria.
