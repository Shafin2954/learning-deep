## The map

You now understand encoders, decoders, and encoder-decoders (F12). Choosing your base
model *is* choosing among those three, filtered by Bengali coverage, fertility (ch. 3),
size (your 8GB card), and license. Pick wrong and you fight the model all month; pick
right and fine-tuning is easy. Here's the map. (Exact "best" models shift monthly —
verify current options on the Hugging Face hub and any Bengali leaderboard before
committing; treat the names below as the stable landmarks.)

## Encoder models: understanding tasks

Bidirectional, no causal mask — best for classification, sentiment, NLI, NER, extractive
QA, and producing embeddings.

- **BanglaBERT** (csebuetnlp) — ELECTRA-style, trained on a large Bengali corpus with the
  csebuetnlp normalizer (ch. 2). Low Bengali fertility, the standard strong baseline for
  Bengali understanding tasks. Small enough to full-fine-tune on 8GB.
- **MuRIL** (Google) — 17 Indian languages incl. Bengali, trained with transliteration
  pairs; strong on code-mixed/transliterated Bengali ("Banglish").
- **IndicBERT** (AI4Bharat), **XLM-RoBERTa** (multilingual, 100 langs) — solid
  multilingual fallbacks; XLM-R-large is a heavier but reliable choice.

## Decoder / generative LLMs: generation tasks

Causal — for open QA, chat, summarization, reasoning, instruction following.

- **TituLLM / TituLM** (Hishab, 2025) — Bengali-focused LLMs ($\approx$1B–3B),
  continue-trained for Bengali; good size/quality fit for your hardware via QLoRA.
- **TigerLLM** (2025) — another recent Bengali-centric LLM line.
- **Multilingual general LLMs with real Bengali coverage:** Llama-3.x (8B), Qwen2.5
  (varied sizes), Gemma-2, Aya (Cohere, explicitly multilingual), BLOOM. These are strong
  and well-supported by the fine-tuning stack (PEFT/Unsloth, ch. 9), but check Bengali
  fertility and quality — general models vary a lot on Bengali.

## Encoder-decoder: seq2seq tasks

For translation and summarization where input and output are both text.

- **BanglaT5** (csebuetnlp) — Bengali seq2seq, the natural choice for Bn$\leftrightarrow$En
  translation and Bengali summarization.
- **mT5 / IndicBART** — multilingual seq2seq fallbacks.

```python
    ("hishab/titulm-1b",      "decoder", 1000,   True,  False),   # QLoRA only
    ("Qwen2.5-7B",            "decoder", 7000,   False, False),   # QLoRA only
]
print(f"{'model':26s} {'arch':9s} {'params(M)':>9s} {'native':>7s} {'fullFT8GB':>10s}")
for name, arch, p, native, fullft in candidates:
    print(f"{name:26s} {arch:9s} {p:>9d} {str(native):>7s} {str(fullft):>10s}")
# Decision rule from the chapter: task -> arch first; then native+low-fertility;
# then size vs 8GB (full-FT small, QLoRA big); then license/offline; start small.
```

The decision order:

1. **Task family decides the architecture.** Understanding $\to$ encoder. Generation $\to$
   decoder. Text-in/text-out transduction (translate/summarize) $\to$ enc-dec (or a
   decoder LLM prompted to do it). This is the single biggest fork; get it right first
   (ch. 7).
2. **Bengali coverage + fertility (ch. 3).** Prefer Bengali-specific or Indic models;
   measure fertility on your data. High fertility = truncation + cost.
3. **Size vs your 8GB card.** Full fine-tune: stick to $\le \approx 300$M (BanglaBERT
   fits). Bigger (1B–8B): QLoRA only (ch. 9). A 7–8B model in 4-bit is the ceiling on
   8GB.
4. **License + reproducibility + Kaggle availability.** The model must be usable under the
   competition rules and loadable in the (often offline) submission environment (ch. 13).
   Check the license and that weights can be pre-downloaded as a Kaggle dataset.
5. **Start small, prove the loop, then scale.** A small encoder baseline you can iterate
   on in minutes beats a 7B model you can train once a day. Earn the right to go big.

The honest default for week 1: understanding task $\to$ **BanglaBERT**, full fine-tune.
Generation task $\to$ a small Bengali or multilingual decoder via **QLoRA**. Translation/
summarization $\to$ **BanglaT5**. Get a submitted baseline with one of these before you
chase anything fancier (ch. 17).
